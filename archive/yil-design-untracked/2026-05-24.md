## Overview

This draft reconciles the 2026-05-24 TIL inbox around one cohesive pattern: good systems preserve raw material, provenance, consent, and deterministic state before trying to synthesize or productize the output.

The day's examples span music rights, agent workflows, context management, and personal-site publishing. The design should make those feel connected without flattening them into a generic “systems thinking” essay. The page should look like a provenance-aware container: raw captures and source links remain visible, then flow through checks for consent, credit, compensation, state, and growth.

## Reconciliation Summary

### Notable learnings
- Spotify is framing fan-made AI covers/remixes as a rights and market-design problem: consent, credit, and compensation for participating artists and songwriters.
- The strongest agent leverage may be shifting from one-off prompts into routines: skills, hooks, subagents, checks, and concise memory.
- Fresh context plus deterministic external state may beat long probabilistic memory; repo state and run records can reduce drift from generated summaries.
- Personal sites may work best as gardens, not streams: some notes should stay short, raw, and visibly unfinished before growing into bigger pieces.
- Dan's own pattern noticed is the connective tissue: create containers that preserve raw material, provenance, and consent before forcing synthesis.

### Possible YIL angles
- “Containers before synthesis” as a reusable lens for AI products, agent workflows, and TIL/YIL publishing.
- Licensed AI remixes show that governance, attribution, and compensation can be the actual product surface.
- Hermes/YIL should rely more on durable run records and source-linked artifacts than on conversational memory.
- A personal site can show notes as growing artifacts rather than forcing every post into either “published essay” or “ephemeral stream.”

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Keep Dan's exact pattern visible: raw material, provenance, and consent before synthesis.
- Let the visual language come from ledgers, containers, source traces, and gardens.
- Treat music rights, agent routines, deterministic state, and personal-site gardens as parallel examples of the same workflow shift.
- Preserve open questions instead of pretending the captures already resolve them.

### Don'ts
- Don't turn this into a generic AI governance page; keep the workflow/product-container angle central.
- Don't imply all notes need to become essays; the garden point is that some artifacts should remain raw and growing.
- Don't publish or mark approved until Dan reviews the artifact.

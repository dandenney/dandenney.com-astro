The pick was Bobby Witt over 0.5 singles, which is the kind of bet this project keeps pretending not to love right until it cashes in the least dramatic way possible. It finished first because it asked for one specific thing, not an entire game script, and Witt delivered it quickly enough with a single while adding a double and a walk for good measure.

The signal was split, but not in a useless way. The final call and Perplexity both landed on Witt. Claude preferred Donovan Clingan under 10.5 rebounds, which also won comfortably at 7. The broader board was where the disagreement actually mattered: Jaylen Brown under 33.5 points plus rebounds won, Crochet under 7.5 strikeouts lost by one, and the Wembanyama cluster did exactly what correlated clusters do when a star decides to score 35 points.

The result was a win, and more importantly a placed win. The 3-loss experiment had Bobby Witt over 0.5 singles and Donovan Clingan under 10.5 rebounds on the card, and both cashed, so there is no doubling ladder to drag into the next day. For once, the martingale math gets to sit quietly in the corner and bother someone else.

The learning is that structural simplicity held up better than correlated conviction. Witt needed one single and got it. Clingan needed to stay under an elevated rebound line and did. The rebound-only Wembanyama under also survived. But once the board stacked multiple Wembanyama scoring-related unders, one usage spike was enough to kill both of them together. That is not bad luck so much as concentrated exposure wearing a jersey.

The verdict is plain. The placed bets aligned with the final recommendation and Claude, and that alignment paid off with a 2-0 experiment day. Perplexity matched the Witt side and won as well, while the extra Wembanyama under exposure accounted for most of the board's losses.

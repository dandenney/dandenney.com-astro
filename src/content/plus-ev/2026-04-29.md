The pick was Tyler Mahle over 1.5 walks allowed. Not glamorous, obviously, but it rose because the board kept circling the same point: two walks is a short trip compared with asking playoff stars to behave normally for forty minutes. Mahle needed a little wildness, not a full game script.

The signal was more interesting than the price. Smart Signal itself preferred Wembanyama under 3.5 blocks, but the broader board tilted toward Mahle. Perplexity and Claude both landed there, and the cross-model average made it the clearest consensus play on the slate. The disagreement was useful. The NBA unders looked sharper in isolation, but they were really one shared thesis wearing multiple hats.

The result was a win. Mahle issued 3 walks, the placed bet cashed, and the 3-loss martingale experiment resets instead of doubling again. The full tracked board still finished 2-4, which is a less inspiring number if you enjoy accuracy more than survival.

The learning is straightforward. This was the 1st `combo-over` tracked this week, and it hit, 1 of 1. More importantly, the consensus prop with the shortest path to cash outperformed the prettier playoff unders. Wembanyama finished with 6 blocks, and Embiid posted 45 PRA with 37 points plus rebounds, so the correlated NBA under family failed together exactly the way correlated ideas tend to fail, all at once and with poor timing.

The verdict is plain enough. The placed bet sided with Perplexity and Claude over the internal top lean, and that alignment paid off. On this slate, consensus on the ugly MLB prop beat confidence in the cleaner-looking NBA under story.

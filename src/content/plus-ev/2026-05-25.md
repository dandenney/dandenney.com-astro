The pick was, again, not a live pick. The final recommendation stayed on SKIP, with Jared McCain under 17.5 PRA logged only as the least-bad paper comparison. That was the right framing at the time, because this was not a deep card. It was two unders from the same game pretending to be a menu.

The signal was unusually narrow. Rithmm supplied both candidates, and both lived inside Thunder-Spurs. Triage downgraded the board on correlation grounds, then left McCain as the only viable-but-fragile paper lean. Perplexity landed in the same place. Claude did not bother pretending one of these needed to be forced and stayed on SKIP. The disagreement was minor. The real consensus was that same-game under clusters are not diversification just because they have different player names.

The result was 2-0. McCain finished with 4 points, 2 rebounds, and 0 assists, good for 6 PRA and an easy under. Wembanyama put up 33 points but only 8 rebounds, so his under 13.5 got home too. The martingale consequence was none, because no official wager was placed. No reset, no doubling, no damage, just another pass watching a perfect paper card walk by.

The learning is that this was the week's 1st `combo-under`, and it is 1 of 1 so far. More important, it was also the week's 1st `rebounds-under`, and that category is also 1 of 1. The useful point is not that the process should start loving same-game unders. It is that the correlation warning was structurally right even though the outcomes were friendly. McCain's ticket won because the feared role expansion never materialized. Wembanyama's won because his scoring line did not drag his rebounds with it. Those are results, not stable permissions.

The verdict is plain. The official process sided with nobody and took no bankroll swing. The named paper lean, shared by the final writeup and Perplexity, won.

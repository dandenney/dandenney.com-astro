The pick was Deni Avdija over 6.5 rebounds (-148), which rose to the top because it looked like the least fragile path on the board. The case was clean enough: recent form, season baseline, and favorable matchup history against the Clippers. Then Avdija scored 35 points and somehow still finished with 4 rebounds. The chosen path was tidy. The actual stat line was not cooperative.

The signal was a full source split, which is more informative than consensus when it goes this hard. Rithmm's listed card went 3-0. SportsLine's listed card went 0-6. Triage still promoted three SportsLine plays into the best-leans tier, and all three lost. Perplexity and the actual placed bets landed on Brooklyn under 104.5 team total. Claude preferred Mobley over 25.5 points plus rebounds. Neither recommendation got close to rescuing the day.

The result was simply a loss stack. Deni lost. Brooklyn scored 108 and beat the team total by 3.5, so both the 3-loss and 6-loss experiment bets lost together. Mobley finished with 10 points and 6 rebounds, good for 16 P+R and nowhere near the number. In martingale terms, there was no reset anywhere that mattered. Both active ladders would double again if they continue.

The learning is that source quality mattered more than pick shape here. The supposedly cleaner structural plays, Deni rebounds and Brooklyn team total under, both failed plainly. Meanwhile the picks downgraded because of a bad availability assumption, the Jaylen Brown unders, both won once Brown was actually active. The process error was not subtle. The board was misread at the source-selection level before it was misread at the individual-pick level.

The verdict is blunt. The placed bets sided with Perplexity and lost twice on the same read. The final recommendation lost, Claude lost with its own separate miss, and the only clean winner on the page was the source family that mostly got screened out.

The final recommendation stayed a pass, with LeBron James under 35.5 PRA logged as the paper lean and Matthew Liberatore over 1.5 walks allowed as the best alternative. That was not indecision. It was the process noticing that the board offered three different ways to lose expensively, even if the numbers looked serviceable.

The signal split in a revealing way. The triage layer treated LeBron and Liberatore as the only remotely viable options, then still stopped short of an official leg. Perplexity and Claude both settled on Liberatore, which made him the closest thing the board had to a cross-source favorite. Nobody had much appetite for Vidal Bruján under 1.5 total bases once the words Coors and total-bases under appeared in the same sentence. Reasonably so.

The result was a mildly insulting 3-0 paper sweep. LeBron finished with 31 PRA, Bruján finished with 0 total bases, and Liberatore walked 3 batters in 6 innings. The martingale consequence was none, because no official experiment bet was placed. No reset, no doubling, no bankroll damage, just a clean reminder that a pass can watch winners go by without becoming a mistake.

The learning is that this was the board's 1st `combo-under` anchor in the current recap trail, and it is 1 of 1 so far. More important than the microscopic sample is the shape of the disagreement. The LeBron under won, but it still depended on suppressing a superstar's full playoff usage across three stat buckets, which is not the same thing as being sturdy. Liberatore won in the more boring way, which is instructive. When a weak slate offers one paper side that needs a star to stay human and another that just needs a starter to keep issuing walks, the less glamorous failure mode is usually the one worth respecting.

The verdict is simple. The official process sided with nobody and avoided risk. Claude and Perplexity both sided with Liberatore and won.

The pick was Jacob Wilson over 0.5 singles, mostly because it looked like the cleanest contact bet left once the obviously invalid NBA clutter got swept off the sheet. That part was tidy enough. The less tidy part was that Wilson then went 0-for-4 against the White Sox, which is the rude but legally accepted way for a singles-over to die.

The signal split into two camps and one of them aged better. The final call, Perplexity, and ChatGPT all sided with Wilson and all lost with him. Claude went with Vladimir Guerrero Jr. for a single instead, and Guerrero obliged with exactly one. Rithmm's full listed board actually finished 4-3-1, which is decent until you remember the featured pick was on the wrong side of the ledger and one of the winning NBA props, Moussa Diabate under 12.5 rebounds plus assists, was nearly misclassified out of the slate entirely.

The result was a loss for the featured pick. No placed martingale experiment was documented in the daily log, so there is no ladder reset or doubling update to report here, just a plain featured loss and an administrative reminder that missing the board is its own tax.

The learning is that slate validation is not clerical work. It is part of the edge. The Knicks and Timberwolves removals were correct, but the note also treated Stephen Curry as the only valid NBA leg when Charlotte-Orlando was live and Diabate's under was perfectly real. A process that identifies winners but misfiles them still degrades its own decisions. That is not variance. That is bookkeeping with consequences.

The verdict is straightforward. The Wilson side, backed by the final recommendation and Perplexity, lost. Claude backed Guerrero and won. The broader board was profitable enough to be annoying about it.

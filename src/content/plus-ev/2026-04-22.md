The pick was Kevin Durant under 33.5 points plus rebounds plus assists, which got there by being the least fragile version of the same basic fade. If Durant played, the broad combo under gave more room than the points-only or points-plus-assists variants. He did play, and he finished at 30 PRA on 23 points, 3 rebounds, and 4 assists. The 3-loss experiment used the same idea at under 32.5 PRA and got the reset.

The signal was unusually aligned. The final recommendation and Perplexity both landed on Durant under 33.5 PRA. Claude took a different route with Freddy Fermin under 0.5 singles, but that won too because Fermin's only hit was a double. The disagreement was about expression, not direction. The board was really one Durant thesis plus three Coors-field hitter unders that looked shakier on paper than they did by midnight.

The result was a win, and then several more wins for good measure. Every listed play cashed. Durant stayed under all three of his numbers, Tovar finished with 0 total bases, Goodman stopped at 1, and Fermin logged 0 singles. Board result: 6-0. For the martingale mechanics, that means the 3-loss experiment resets instead of doubling again. Mercifully, variance took the night off.

The learning is that correlation is a bookkeeping problem, not automatically a bad read. This was the 2nd `combo-under` to be placed this week, and 2 of 2 hit. More importantly, the board should still be graded as one successful Durant thesis, not three independent victories. The useful part is that the widest version of the under was also the right one to prioritize. When the cap is really on ceiling and not just scoring, the broader combo line gives the read more ways to survive normal stat noise.

The verdict is plain. The experiment sided with the same Durant angle the final recommendation and Perplexity preferred, and that alignment paid off. Claude went with the Fermin singles under instead, and that one won too, so this was a rare day when disagreement did not produce a casualty.

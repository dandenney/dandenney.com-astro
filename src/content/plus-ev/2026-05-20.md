The pick was Reds +1.5 at -167, which is not a glamorous sentence but was the whole point. This board was split between two acceptable team sides and two pitcher strikeout unders dressed up as safer than they were. The Reds run line finished first because it carried the strongest survival structure, not because it was cheap.

The signal was unusually clean. The final recommendation, Perplexity, and Claude all landed on the same side. Triage kept Twins moneyline in the viable bucket, but behind the Reds because a one-run loss still cashes the run line and still buries the moneyline. The two pitcher-K unders stayed off the board for the right pregame reasons, even if both later cooperated.

The result was a win. Cincinnati beat Philadelphia 4-1, so the experiment bet cashed without needing the +1.5 insurance at all. That resets the martingale series instead of doubling the next stake, which is a much less theatrical and much healthier outcome.

The learning is that this was the week's 1st `runline`, and it is 1 of 1 so far. More useful than the tiny sample is where the board's lone loss lived. The backup team side lost 2-1, while the official run-line favorite won outright. The two rejected pitcher-K unders also won, which is annoying but not corrective. Process is not disproved every time a fragile prop survives one night. Structure still matters more than retrospective smugness.

The verdict is plain enough. The placed bet sided with the final model consensus and won. On this board, following the same answer as Perplexity and Claude paid off.

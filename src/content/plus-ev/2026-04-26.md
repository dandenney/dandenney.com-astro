The pick was Nikola Jokić under 14.5 rebounds, mostly because the model gap looked cleaner than the rest of the board and the slate did not offer a true high-conviction favorite. That logic aged poorly. Jokić finished with 15 rebounds, which is an annoying way of saying the number was never as far from his live playoff range as the projection made it look.

The signal was split in a way that mattered. The final review chose the Jokić rebounds under. Perplexity landed on Shane McClanahan under 5.5 strikeouts. Claude preferred Ernie Clement over 0.5 singles. The broader board was messy, but the practical divide was simple: the NBA superstar unders looked sharp on paper, while the cleaner MLB one-stat prop asked for a single and nothing more exotic.

The result was a loss on the featured pick, but not on the experiment that actually put money at risk. Clement got there with 1 single and 1 double in Toronto's 5-3 win over Cleveland, so the 3-loss martingale experiment cashed and resets instead of doubling again. The rest of the board was a small demolition project, finishing 2-6 overall.

The learning is that this was the 1st `rebounds-under` result of the week, and it lost, while the day also reinforced how fragile playoff superstar unders become when the line already sits inside the player's recent series range. Jokić had already posted back-to-back 15-rebound games, so under 14.5 was not buying much real cushion. By contrast, the singles prop still carried its usual nuisance risk, but it required one correctly shaped hit and got exactly that.

The verdict is plain enough. The final recommendation lost, Perplexity lost, and Claude won. On the only placed bet, siding with Claude instead of the featured pick paid for the day and stopped the progression from getting more expensive.

The pick was no pick at all. The final stance was to skip, not because the board looked awful, but because it looked thin: two correlated Jokić unders, one middling Twins run line, and no true high-confidence angle worth pretending was safer than it was. Discipline got the nod over action, which is noble right up until the board goes perfect without you.

The signal was quietly stronger than the pass implied. Claude liked Jokić under 23.5 rebounds plus assists. Perplexity preferred Jokić under 53.5 PRA. Gemini took Twins +1.5. That is not full consensus, but it is a clean structural alignment, because every live recommendation leaned toward lower-output or protected-game-script outcomes. The disagreement was over which expression of the same general thesis was least fragile.

The result was a sweep. Twins +1.5 cashed in a 7-1 Minnesota win, and Jokić finished with 28 points, 9 rebounds, and 10 assists, which meant both unders won as well. The martingale consequence was nothing, because nothing was placed. The series did not reset, and it did not double. It simply watched a 3-0 board from the sideline, which is emotionally irritating and mathematically allowed.

The learning is narrower than the regret. This was a `combo-under` result day on the tracked board, and both combo unders hit, 2 of 2. More important, the slate settled coherently. The Jokić props were correlated and won together because Minnesota produced the exact lower-ceiling environment the under case needed. That does not prove the skip was wrong. It proves a thin board can still be directionally correct, which is less comforting than it sounds.

The verdict is plain. The experiment followed no recommender because the final call was to pass, and that caution cost it a clean winning day on paper. Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini all sided with playable outcomes, and all of them got paid.

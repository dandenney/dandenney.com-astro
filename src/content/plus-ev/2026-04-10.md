The pick was Kansas City moneyline (-178), which reached the top because it looked like the least annoying path on the board. A cleaner team-side pitching edge, less fragility than a player prop, fewer ways to lose than a combo under. Then the Royals got shut out 2-0. The market was simpler. The result was not kinder.

The signal split in a useful way. The final recommendation landed on Kansas City. Perplexity went to Randy Vasquez over 4.5 strikeouts and got paid with 8 in 5.2 innings. Claude preferred RJ Barrett under 30.5 PRA and lost by the smallest amount that still counts, 31. Rithmm did very little besides produce one loss and one void. SportsLine, for all its clutter, actually carried the listed board at 6-2-1 with one void. The page looked messy. The wins were not especially shy.

The result was a split card for the actual experiments. The 3-loss branch placed Randy Vasquez over 4.5 strikeouts and won, so that ladder resets. The 6-loss branch placed Barrett under 30.5 PRA and lost, which means the next stake doubles if the sequence continues. Kansas City, the featured pick, simply lost outright. Two other listed props, Joel Embiid under 31.5 points plus assists and Kobe Brown under 21.5 PRA, voided because neither player logged game stats.

The learning is that structural cleanliness still needs an actual scoring path. Kansas City was promoted because team sides tend to be less fragile than player props. Fine in theory. In this case the more durable signal was the workload-based pitching prop that triage had already started distrusting late. Vasquez did not need elegance. He needed six-ish innings and a free-swinging opponent, and the Rockies cooperated. Barrett under is the other lesson. A combo under with a thin cushion can die on a perfectly ordinary 22/6/3 line.

The verdict is plain enough. Perplexity's recommendation won and the 3-loss experiment followed it to a reset. The final recommendation lost, Claude lost with it on the 6-loss branch, and the day managed to turn the "safe" side into the most efficient miss on the page.

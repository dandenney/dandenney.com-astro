VN Pho is a clean, straightforward pho stop where the bowl does the talking. The rare steak pho came out with a clear, deeply savory broth and plenty of fresh garnish, and the red Thai tea was an easy pairing that kept each sip and bite in balance.

The broth had that focused beef flavor you want from a good pho: warm, clean, and not muddy. Thin slices of rare steak softened in the heat without turning tough, so each bite stayed tender. A handful of bean sprouts added crispness, while jalapeño slices brought bright heat that you could dial up as you went.

The red Thai tea played the opposite role in a good way. It was sweet and creamy enough to cool things down between bites, especially once the jalapeño heat built up. Together, it made the meal feel complete without getting heavy.

If you want a no-drama pho spot on Charlotte Pike, VN Pho delivers where it counts: hot broth, tender steak, fresh add-ins, and a drink that rounds it out.

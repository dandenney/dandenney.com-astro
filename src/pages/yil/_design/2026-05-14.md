## Overview

This page ports BMW's corporate site visual language — measured, precision-engineered, editorial — onto a day of conference notes about structured AI work. The fit is intentional: BMW corporate and Nashville AI Week's dominant theme share the same DNA. Both are about engineered precision over raw confidence, documented systems over improvised speed, and humans in the loop to apply judgment that machines cannot supply.

The BMW reference (bmw.com) supplies everything structural: white canvas (#ffffff) as the base surface, dark navy (#1a2129) for hero and quote bands only, BMW Blue (#1c69d4) as the single primary accent with a scale of deeper blues across sections, Inter 700/300 as the heavy-display/light-body editorial pair, rectangular 0px corners everywhere, UPPERCASE letter-spaced section labels ("SKILLS", "STRUCTURE", "DECISION SCIENCE", "DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE"), and 80px section rhythm. No drop shadows — depth comes entirely from color-block contrast between the white canvas bands and the two dark navy interruptions (hero + quote).

What makes this day distinct from a generic blog post is the discipline/structure frame expressed through the BMW system: the spec-cell stats in the discipline section (2,191 tests / 109 releases / $450) mirror BMW's configurator data tables; the UPPERCASE section labels carry the "machined precision" voice that fits the conference's core claim; and the typography's 700→300 weight contrast between headers and body mirrors the theme's core argument — the display layer (the LLM) needs the quiet support of carefully designed structure beneath it.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Hold every button, card, and input to 0px border-radius — the rectangular silhouette IS the precision signal
- Run section labels in Inter 700 uppercase with 1.5px letter-spacing, mirroring BMW's "LEARN MORE" inline link voice
- Let the two dark navy bands (hero + quote) do all the contrast work — every other section stays on white or soft-grey canvas, no additional dark surfaces

### Don'ts
- Don't add pill badges or rounded corners, even for the section label chips — the BMW source explicitly forbids it
- Don't use the same section surface back-to-back — the rhythm alternates canvas → canvas (with card insets) → dark band → canvas → canvas; repeating a surface kills the BMW section rhythm
- Don't add drop shadows to any card or surface — depth comes from color contrast and the dark navy interruptions, never from shadow

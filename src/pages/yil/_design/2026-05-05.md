## Overview

This page ports HP.com's commercial-catalog visual language onto the quietest possible day — a Discord thread check-in with no links, no code, and no headline insight. The pairing is intentional: HP's system is built for systematic, unhurried catalog navigation, not editorial drama. A sparse entry deserves a clean, open surface. The page follows HP's full surface rhythm (white canvas → ink-slab quote band → cloud alternating band → white), with Electric Blue reserved for the two chevron decorations and the single section pill — at most two blue signal elements per viewport, exactly as the HP system specifies.

Source: HP.com design-md analysis (2026-05-05-design-md.md). The font is Manrope 400/500 — HP's recommended open-source substitute for the proprietary Forma DJR Micro. All display headlines run at weight 500 with line-height 1.0, per HP's unusual but deliberate choice to keep even hero text open and approachable rather than commanding.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Reserve HP Electric Blue (#024ad8) strictly for the pill label, section underline bar, and the two hero chevron decorations — never as a section fill or card background
- Set all headlines at Manrope 500 with line-height 1.0, including the hero — resist increasing weight even at large sizes
- Let the whitespace breathe; the sparse content IS the content, and compressing it with artificial padding or extra sections would undermine the HP commercial-clean aesthetic

### Don'ts
- Don't introduce a second chromatic accent — this day has one theme and the HP system reserves blue as the single signal color
- Don't round the chevron decorations — the 0px-radius angular slash echoes the HP wordmark; a softened chevron is just a generic shape
- Don't add a references section — the inbox has no links, and an empty references block is worse than no references block

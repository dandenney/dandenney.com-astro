## Overview

This design ports Airbnb's visual language — pure white canvas (#ffffff), near-black ink (#222222), and restrained single-accent discipline — to a day centered on conceptual frameworks about information and interface design. The reference's single-accent principle (Rausch #ff385c for primary everything) is adapted into three accent hues that each track one of the day's distinct themes: Rausch for the capacity/energy news, Luxe Purple for IA's intellectual depth, and Plus Magenta for the Execution→Judgment shift. What makes this day distinct from a generic blog post is the structural tension — the AI infrastructure news is context, while the IA and Judgment UI sections are the real payload — and the design makes that hierarchy legible through surface rhythm (news on surface, ideas on white) without spelling it out. Airbnb's typographic restraint (modest 500–700 weights, no display heroics) fits a day where the ideas carry the weight, not the layout.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Keep the canvas 90% white + ink; let Rausch, violet, and magenta appear only at accent moments — pill labels, card borders, section category tags, and the quote band's single accent line
- Use Airbnb's one shadow tier (`rgba(0,0,0,0.04) 0 2px 6px, rgba(0,0,0,0.1) 0 4px 8px`) on the judgment-ui callout card and the two infrastructure cards — nowhere else
- Round every card and interactive element at 8px minimum; pill-shape the section category tags fully (9999px radius) — no hard corners except the page grid itself

### Don'ts
- Don't use a dark canvas for any section except the standalone quote band; Airbnb has no dark mode on public pages — the single dark moment earns its drama by isolation
- Don't size the hero headline past the clamp ceiling or weight it above 700 — the ideas are the authority, not the letterforms; trust the white space
- Don't present the HubSpot seven-screens example as a numbered step list — it is an illustration of an anti-pattern, not a process to follow; keep it inside the callout card as a single paragraph

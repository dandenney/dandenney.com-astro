## Overview

This design ports Apple's visual language — pure white (#ffffff), parchment (#f5f5f7), and near-black tile (#272729) surface alternation, a single Action Blue (#0066cc) interactive accent, and the signature SF Pro / system-ui typographic cadence — to a day where every lesson is about rethinking defaults. The reference's defining discipline is that surface color change IS the section divider: no borders, no gradient bands, no decorative chrome. On light surfaces, Action Blue #0066cc handles all links and labels; on the two dark tile sections (quote band, media-as-code), Sky Link Blue #2997ff steps in exactly as Apple's design system specifies — the accent isn't a brand choice, it's a legibility rule. What makes this page distinct from a generic tech blog post is the symmetry in the layout: the page opens with white and closes with parchment, and two dark tiles (quote + media) act as visual anchors at roughly the 1/6 and 5/6 mark, turning the content hierarchy into a rhythm you feel before you read it. The "prompting" split section — two dark code-window cards on a parchment field — is the closest this page comes to Apple's product-render-on-surface moment: the two prompts are the artifact, and the parchment is the pedestal.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Use system-ui / -apple-system at weight 600 and negative letter-spacing (-0.28px) for all headlines; the "Apple tight" cadence is what makes the type feel confident without shouting
- Let the surface color changes do the work of section dividers — no additional border, gradient, or decorative line between sections; the tile edges meet at 0 gap
- Apply Sky Link Blue #2997ff exclusively on the two dark tile sections (quote band and media-as-code); use Action Blue #0066cc for every interactive element on white and parchment surfaces

### Don'ts
- Don't introduce a second brand accent color; any pill label, CTA, inline link, or category tag that lives on a light surface should be Action Blue #0066cc — full stop
- Don't round or add margin to the full-bleed tile sections; they are edge-to-edge rectangles and the color change IS the border
- Don't set body copy below 17px or above weight 400; Apple's body register reads at exactly 17px/400/1.47 line-height, and the extra pixel over 16px defines the reading pace this design depends on

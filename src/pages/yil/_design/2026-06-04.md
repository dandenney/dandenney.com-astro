## Overview

The design ports Coinbase's institutional marketing visual language — near-monochromatic white canvas, single-accent scarcity, editorial weight-400 display type, and dark full-bleed band rotation — to a day of notes that share the same calm confidence: big structural shifts stated without urgency. Coinbase Blue (#0052ff) anchors the AI org-design section because it reads as institutional and strategic; the brand's accent yellow (#f4b000) fits the judgment/taste section's illustrative, creative character; trading green (#05b169) marks production as "winning" (it is up, literally); and trading red (#cf202f) frames structured multi-agent disagreement as a designed feature rather than a flaw. The page's most distinctive visual moment mirrors Coinbase's signature: a full-bleed near-black (#0a0b0d) band carrying the "laughably stale" quote, followed by the production-canvas callout — two dark bands separated by light editorial sections, just as Coinbase alternates white and dark editorial heroes.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Keep the hero headline at font-weight 400 — the day's insights are calm observations, not trading-platform urgency; heaviness would undercut the tone.
- Use Coinbase Blue exactly once as an action/emphasis color outside the ai-org-design section — scarcity is the rule; every extra blue moment dilutes it.
- Let the full-bleed dark bands do the heavy lifting for visual contrast — the white sections should feel genuinely airy with 96px vertical padding.

### Don'ts
- Don't use trading green (#05b169) or trading red (#cf202f) as button backgrounds — these are text-color-only semantic signals in the Coinbase system; they should remain text accents on white or card surfaces only.
- Don't bold the display headline — the entire point of the weight-400 choice is editorial calm; a bold hero breaks the Coinbase-derived voice.
- Don't introduce a fifth accent color or pull from outside the Coinbase palette — the four accents (blue, yellow, green, red) already span the full system; adding more fragments the cohesion.

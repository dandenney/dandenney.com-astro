## Overview

This design should feel like an editorial notebook about structure, provenance, and unfinished growth — not a futuristic AI dashboard. The day links four domains that all point to the same product insight: value comes from the container around the intelligence. In music, that container is licensing and compensation. In coding agents, it is the routine and harness. In memory, it is deterministic external state. In publishing, it is a garden-shaped note system that preserves rough material before polishing it.

The visual system should therefore privilege framed sections, ruled boxes, narrow columns, and calm paper-like surfaces. Warm neutrals support the "notes first" mood, while violet, teal, and orange separate the four themes without making the page feel overdesigned. The structure should gradually move from governed/productized systems into a more personal publishing reflection, so the final section opens up and breathes.

## Reconciliation Summary

### Notable learnings
- Licensed AI remixes are becoming a governance product, not just a model capability story: consent, credit, and compensation define the usable surface.
- Agent leverage is shifting from prompt cleverness to reusable routines: hooks, skills, memory, checks, and harnesses.
- External state and machine-readable run records can be more trustworthy than long conversational memory when reconstructing context.
- Personal publishing works better when some notes stay raw and visibly in-progress rather than getting forced into polished stream content too early.

### Dominant themes
- The real moat is increasingly the wrapper around intelligence: rules, provenance, validation, and repeatable workflow.
- Preserve raw material first; synthesis should happen later and with traceability.
- Dan's own TIL→YIL pipeline is not separate from the day's thesis — it is one instance of it.

### Possible YIL angles
- "The product is the governance layer."

- What UI best signals that a note is still growing rather than finished?

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Keep the page editorial and notebook-like: ruled lines, framed containers, and generous white space over glossy AI chrome.
- Let each section feel like a different kind of container — policy card, routine diagram, state panel, garden note — while staying inside one warm visual family.
- Make the final garden section visibly softer and less boxed-in than the earlier systems sections so the page lands on openness rather than control.

### Don'ts
- Do not turn the page into a generic "AI future" dashboard with fake charts or arbitrary technical spectacle.
- Do not collapse all four source ideas into one vague model-vs-product thesis; preserve the distinct examples because their parallelism is the insight.
- Do not over-polish the garden theme into certainty; some of the point is that unfinished notes remain unfinished on purpose.

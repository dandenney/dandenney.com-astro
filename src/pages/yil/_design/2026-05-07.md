## Overview

This day's visual personality is warm and editorial — a personal learning journal entry, not a tech blog post. The cream/stone surface and serif headline communicate "someone thinking out loud" rather than "documentation." Three distinct accent colors (amber, indigo, emerald) give each section its own identity without fighting each other, and the dark-slate quote band provides the one moment of genuine contrast, anchoring Joe Liemandt's line as the emotional center of the page. The layout is deliberately unhurried: full-width prose opening, generous whitespace, and a steps layout for the only genuinely step-based content (the Google Slides workflow). The through-line — things with unexpected depth underneath — should feel implicit in the structure, not stated again in every section.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Use the serif headline family for section headers to maintain the editorial warmth throughout, not just at the hero
- Let the Google Slides section be the most structured thing on the page — the steps layout earns its numbered cards because the content IS a numbered process
- Give the Alpha School cards breathing room; each insight (press conference, contract negotiation, top 1%, personal reflection) is punchy enough to stand alone as a card — don't collapse them into one block of prose

### Don'ts
- Don't add decorative numbers to the section headers — the three topics are parallel observations, not a sequence, and numbering them implies a ranking or order that isn't there
- Don't use the dark-slate quote band color anywhere else on the page — it should feel like a one-time tonal shift, not a recurring element
- Don't over-style the Remix section; it's the lightest note of the day and should read quickly — prose layout, no cards, no steps, just a clean observation with a link

## Overview

This page ports claude.com's warm editorial visual language — cream canvas, slab-serif display, coral + teal + amber accents, cream-to-dark surface rhythm — onto a day of systems thinking about how artifacts and boundaries create reliable workflows. The pairing is intentional: claude.com itself embodies the "reliability by construction" principle with its disciplined design system, so using that language to describe the day's learning creates a resonant visual metaphor.

Surface rhythm follows the reference: cream hero → dark-navy quote band → cream feature-card section (specs) → light-cream-card section (memory) → cream-card prose section (explicit beats implied) → dark footer. The hero's right column carries a dark navy code-window card showing a DESIGN.md fragment — the artifact the day's insight is literally about.

Typography is Cormorant Garamond (open-source Copernicus substitute) at weight 400 with negative letter-spacing for display. Inter or system-ui for body. JetBrains Mono for file paths, slash commands, and the code-window card.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- Use monospace for all file paths, directory names, and slash commands (`/yil-design`, `_design/`, `import.meta.glob`) — they are artifacts in the day's story, not prose
- Maintain the cream-to-dark surface rhythm: never place two cream bands back-to-back without a dark or cream-card band between them
- Keep display headlines at weight 400 with negative letter-spacing — this is the editorial voice; boldness breaks it

### Don'ts
- Don't replace the slab-serif with a sans-serif display — Inter for headlines would strip the literary character the reference is built on
- Don't use coral for every accent — it belongs on the specs/artifacts theme (accent-1) and the hero CTA; teal and amber own the other sections
- Don't add a dot-grid or geometric pattern over the cream canvas — the warmth of the cream surface is the texture; layering noise over it dilutes it

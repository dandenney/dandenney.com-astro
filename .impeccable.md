# .impeccable.md

## Design Context

### Users
Peer devs and designers who already know or are discovering Dan's work. They're visiting to read longform content, browse tinkerings, or check out music/restaurant takes. The context is casual and lateral — not evaluative. They're not judging a portfolio; they're reading something a colleague made.

### Brand Personality
Minimal, bold, lighthearted. The site should feel like a thoughtful person made it — not a brand, not a showcase. Trust and camaraderie are the emotional targets. Visitors should feel like they landed somewhere real.

### Aesthetic Direction
**References:** Vercel, Stripe — modern SaaS clarity: generous whitespace, confident typography, restrained color. But with a deliberate tilt toward larger fonts and longform reading comfort. The SaaS influence is a starting point, not a ceiling.

**Anti-reference:** The stereotypical design engineer site — no gratuitous demos, no "look what CSS can do" for its own sake, no over-engineered portfolio vibes, no dark-only terminal aesthetic. Personality comes from restraint and voice, not decoration.

**Theme:** Light and dark mode both supported. Light is the primary reading context; dark should feel equally resolved, not just inverted.

**Color:** Primary accent is `#b51f24` (deep red). Slate neutrals for backgrounds. The custom avatar's teal-green highlight (`#03ef62`) is a secondary accent that appears sparingly. The existing diagonal hatching motif and animated link underlines are established brand details — preserve and refine rather than replace.

**Typography:** Montserrat is the brand font. Size up boldly — lean toward the larger end when in doubt. Longform content should be unhurried and readable at scale.

### Design Principles

1. **Substance over spectacle.** Every visual flourish must earn its place by serving the content or the reader. If it would impress a design Twitter audience but slow down a reader, cut it.

2. **Peer-to-peer, not presenter-to-audience.** Design decisions should feel collegial. Avoid anything that positions the site as a stage. Headers, copy, and hierarchy should feel like a smart person talking, not a brand broadcasting.

3. **Bold type as a first-class element.** Typography is the primary design tool. Use scale, weight, and spacing aggressively before reaching for color or decoration.

4. **Restrained delight.** The diagonal grid hatching, gradient link reveals, and hover animations are the right model: unexpected on close inspection, invisible when you're just reading. Micro-interactions should reward attention without demanding it.

5. **Confident whitespace.** Empty space is not a problem to solve. Resist the urge to fill. SaaS-grade breathing room throughout.

## Plus EV Section Context

### Identity
Plus EV is a standalone sub-section with its own visual identity — dark-only, emerald accent — modeled after the martingale-tracker page. It does not inherit the main site's red accent or dual-mode system. It should feel like a project that lives inside dandenney.com, not a detached product.

### Ownership framing
Dan owns the project; Gale (an AI persona) writes the daily recaps. This split must be clear on the index page — Dan is the experimenter, Gale is the narrator. Neither should be hidden.

### Visual reference
The martingale-tracker page sets the register: font-black headlines, monospace micro-labels with extreme tracking, backdrop-blur cards, emerald/cyan glows, tactical grid overlay. Plus EV should match that energy — bold, data-forward — without being a copy.

### Common failure modes to avoid
- Low contrast labels (text-slate-600 on dark bg is invisible)
- Undersized numbers in stat cards
- No hero weight — the page needs a strong opening, not just a title
- Missing site navigation — Plus EV should link back to dandenney.com
